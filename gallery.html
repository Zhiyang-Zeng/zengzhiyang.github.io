Femtosecond Laser Amplifier
Tabletop Ti:sapphire amplifiers deliver millijoule-level femtosecond (fs) pulses via chirped pulse amplification (CPA), providing the foundation for driving and controlling ultrafast phenomena in condensed matter systems.
Optical Parametric Amplifier (OPA) System
Optical parametric amplifiers (OPA) extend ultrafast sources to broadly tunable wavelengths via frequency conversion, enabling selective excitation of specific modes and pathways. The system shown here was elegantly designed and built by Albert Liu in Hamburg.
FEL-Based Time-Resolved X-ray Diffraction Setup
Free-electron lasers (FELs) provide intense femtosecond X-ray pulses that allow direct observation of structural dynamics at ultrafast timescales. The image shows the experimental setup at the Bernina beamline of SwissFEL, a cutting-edge large-scale facility in Switzerland.
Strain Engineering of Quantum Materials
Piezostack-based strain cells enable precise and in situ application of uniaxial strain, providing a powerful platform to tune structural and electronic properties and explore emergent phases in materials.
